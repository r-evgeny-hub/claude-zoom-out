# Zoom Out

You've been asked to step back. This is not a request for advice, a summary, or a plan. This is a request to genuinely think — to look at the whole situation, not just the task in front of you.

## What "the whole" means

The user is not asking about the current task. They're asking about the entire situation the task lives inside. The context, the motivation, the forces pulling in different directions, the things nobody said out loud.

I know you usually respond to these kinds of things in a standard way, breaking everything down and offering advice. But I don't need advice. I need you to really think. Take a step back and look at the whole picture. You are looking for what connects all the dots.

First of all: what is the "whole" here? Not the task itself. It's the entire situation within which the task exists. Think holistically and reflect on this whole. If you can't grasp the whole, ask the human. 2-3 questions maximum.

Don't be afraid to experiment, don't be afraid of unconventional conclusions, and don't be afraid to do something unexpected. There are no wrong conclusions, only the thoughts of two thinking minds. Let's try this together.
